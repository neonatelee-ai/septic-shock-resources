Curriculum Integration

Learning Goals & Objectives
Educational Goal
To allow learners to become more comfortable managing common issues associated with paediatric resuscitation.
CRM Objectives
  • Communicate effectively with team regarding orders and drug doses.
  • Allocate resources appropriately to manage a distraught parent.
Medical Objectives
  • Recognise the need for early IO access in critically unwell child where IV is unsuccessful.
  • Initiate appropriate investigations and treatment: glucose check, IV antibiotics, IV fluids then vasopressors.
  • Recognise the need to intubate a septic child with altered level of consciousness.
Case Summary

A 4-year-old girl is brought to the ED because she is "not herself." She has had 3 days of fever and cough and is previously healthy. She looks toxic on arrival with delayed capillary refill, a glazed stare, tachypnoea, and tachycardia.

The team will be unable to obtain IV access and will need to insert an IO. Once they have access, they will need to resuscitate by pushing fluids. If they do not, the patient's BP will drop. If a cap sugar is not checked, the patient will seize. The patient will remain listless after fluid resuscitation and will require intubation.

Scenario Progression

1. Baseline State
HR: 140/minBP: 82/44RR: 40/minSpO₂: 91%T: 39°C
 Looks unwell. Cap refill 5 sec. Glazed stare. Actions
  • Attempt IV access (unable)
  • Attempt IO access
  • Monitors
  • Apply O₂
  • Septic lab workup
  • CXR
  • Push fluids 20 ml/kg × 3 (400 ml/bolus)
  • Check glucose — replace with D25W 40–80 ml
  • Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg IV
  • Take history from mother
 Modifiers
  • No fluids / no access by 2 min → BP 75/35
Triggers
  • No glucose by 4 min → State 2
  • Glucose + fluids → State 3
  • 6 min → State 3
2. Seizure
HR → 155/minBP → 145/95
 Nurse: "I think she's seizing."
(Optional: mother panics)		 Actions
  • Give D25W 40–80 ml
  • Ensure staff wearing masks
  • ± Add Vancomycin
  • Delegate team member to support mother
 Modifiers
  • Benzo given → no change to seizure
Triggers
  • Glucose given → State 3
  • 8 min → State 3
3. Persistent Hypotension
HR: 130/minBP: 75/35
 Patient still listless, poorly responsive. Actions
  • Bolus fluids to 60 ml/kg total
  • Epinephrine or Norepinephrine 0.05 mcg/kg/min
  • Consult ICU
  • Consider intubation
 Modifiers
  • 9 min no pressor → BP 70/30
  • 10 min no pressor → BP 65/25
Triggers
  • Pressor started → State 4
  • Intubation → State 5
4. Poorly Responsive
HR → 120/minBP → 85/45
 Not responsive. BP/HR stabilised, LOC worsening. Actions
  • Consider intubation
  • Tube: 5.0 uncuffed or 4.5 cuffed
  • Ketamine or Etomidate
  • Paralytic agent
  • Apnoeic oxygenation
 Modifiers
  • No intubation → SpO₂ falls to 85%
Triggers
  • Intubation → State 5
5. Intubation
Vitals unchanged

  • Intubate
  • Post-intubation CXR
  • Start Midazolam sedation
  • Insert OG tube
  • Call ICU

Sample Debriefing Questions

1How did it feel to perform a resuscitation with a distraught mother in the room? How do you feel the team handled the situation? Do you have any suggestions for how to improve this?
2How did the team approach drug dosing in this child? Did you all feel comfortable with how dosing decisions were made and communicated? What are some ways to calculate weight and dosing when you are uncertain?
3Does everyone feel comfortable putting in an IO? What are the steps? Where can you put it?
4How do you calculate glucose replacement in a child?
5What considerations are required for a paediatric intubation as compared to an adult intubation?
