Emergency Management

Paediatric Septic Shock
Scenario-Based Clinical Algorithm

Patient: Rebecca, 4 yr · 20 kg
HR 140/min
BP 82/44
RR 40/min
SpO₂ 91%
Temp 39°C
Glucose 2.4 mmol/L
Presentation: 4-year-old girl, 3 days of fever & cough, now lethargic and "not herself." Glazed stare, tachypnoeic, tachycardic. Brought directly to resus. IV access unobtainable. Peripheral glucose critically low.
Recognition — Signs of Septic Shock

Cardiovascular

  • Tachycardia HR 140/min
  • Hypotension BP 82/44
  • Cap refill >5 sec (central)
  • Sunken eyes / poor perfusion

Respiratory & CNS

  • Tachypnoea RR 40/min
  • SpO₂ 91% on room air
  • Glazed, lethargic appearance
  • Altered level of consciousness

Metabolic

  • Fever 39°C (source: pneumonia)
  • Hypoglycaemia 2.4 mmol/L
  • No urine output today
  • Rhonchi right lung (CXR: pneumonia)
Scenario Progression & Key Decision Points
1
Baseline State — Initial Resuscitation
HR 140 BP 82/44 SpO₂ 91%

Actions Required

  • Attempt IV access (expect failure)
  • Insert IO — do not delay beyond 2 min
  • Apply O₂ (non-rebreather mask)
  • Attach monitors: ECG, NIBP, SpO₂
  • Check glucose NOW — 2.4 mmol/L
  • Septic lab workup + CXR
  • Push 20 ml/kg × 3 = 400 ml per bolus
  • Antibiotics: Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg IV
  • Take history from mother

Consequences & Triggers

  • No IO by 2 min → BP drops to 75/35
  • No glucose check by 4 min → STATE 2 (Seizure)
  • Glucose checked + fluids given → STATE 3
  • 6 min elapsed → STATE 3
⚠ Glucose Replacement:
2–4 ml/kg of D25W = 40–80 ml IO/IV
2
Seizure — Hypoglycaemic Complication
HR 155 BP 145/95

Actions Required

  • Give glucose immediately (D25W 40–80 ml)
  • Do NOT reach for benzodiazepine first — treat cause
  • Ensure staff in droplet precautions (masks)
  • Consider adding Vancomycin (CSF penetration)
  • Delegate team member: support/inform mother

Consequences & Triggers

  • Benzo given → seizure does NOT stop (glucose is cause)
  • Glucose given → STATE 3
  • 8 min elapsed → STATE 3
3
Persistent Hypotension — Vasopressor Decision
HR 130 BP 75/35

Actions Required

  • Continue fluid boluses up to total 60 ml/kg
  • Start vasopressor via IO
  • Epinephrine 0.05 mcg/kg/min or
  • Norepinephrine 0.05 mcg/kg/min
  • Consult ICU
  • Consider intubation for altered LOC

Consequences & Triggers

  • No pressor by 9 min → BP 70/30
  • No pressor by 10 min → BP 65/25
  • Pressor started → STATE 4
  • Intubation decision → STATE 5
4
Poorly Responsive — Intubation Planning
HR 120 BP 85/45 LOC ↓↓

Actions Required

  • Commit to intubation decision
  • Tube size: 5.0 uncuffed or 4.5 cuffed
  • Induction: Ketamine or Etomidate
  • Paralytic agent
  • Apnoeic oxygenation

Consequences & Triggers

  • No intubation decision → SpO₂ falls to 85%
  • Intubation → STATE 5
Tube sizing (paediatric):
Uncuffed: (Age/4) + 4 = 5.0
Cuffed: (Age/4) + 3.5 = 4.5
5
Intubation & Post-Intubation Care
RR→0 after paralytic

Actions Required

  • Intubate (as planned above)
  • Confirm with post-intubation CXR
  • Start sedation: Midazolam
  • Insert orogastric tube (OG)
  • Call ICU for transfer

End Points

  • Successful intubation → END CASE
  • 12 minutes elapsed → END CASE

🧪 Drug Reference — Weight 20 kg

Ceftriaxone
50 mg/kg = 1000 mg IV
D25W (glucose)
2–4 ml/kg = 40–80 ml IO/IV
Fluid bolus
20 ml/kg = 400 ml (×3 max 60 ml/kg)
Epinephrine
0.05 mcg/kg/min infusion
Norepinephrine
0.05 mcg/kg/min infusion
Midazolam (post-intub.)
Titrate per institutional protocol
Vancomycin (if meningitis)
Consider for CSF penetration

👥 Crisis Resource Management

📢
Clear communication: Verbalise all orders and drug doses. Close the loop — receiver repeats back before administration.
👩
Distraught parent: Designate one team member solely to support and update the mother. Remove from procedural area if necessary.
💉
IO access: Do not persist with IV beyond 2 minutes in a critically unwell child. IO is safe, fast and effective.
🧮
Weight estimation: Use Broselow tape or formula: weight = 2 × (age + 4) = 16 kg (actual 20 kg — use actual if known).
🔄
Reassess frequently: Vital signs, LOC, and glucose must be re-checked after every intervention.
